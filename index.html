The RV8 Disc Golf Challenge

Eight courses in one day.
Do you have what it takes to complete the RV8?

The RV8 Challenge is the ultimate physical and mental disc golf test. The RV8 Challenge is modeled after the success of North Carolina’s “Great 8” and is designed to showcase the variety and challenge of disc golf courses in the Richmond, Virginia area.

This year will serve as the planning year for the RV8. As such, there is no official tournament date. Instead, participants will complete the RV8 Challenge in the month-long time frame between ___ and ___ . The organizers of this event, William Berry and Ben Moxley, are completing the RV8 challenge on Saturday, July 2nd 2016. If you would like to take the challenge on the same day as us, just email us and let us know.

There are no entry fees or prizes for the RV8 Challenge, merely good times and bragging rights. This may change in years to come as we refine and expand the event to model a tournament format.

Scoring:

The RV8 is a captain’s choice doubles event. Each partner throws from the teepad and the team chooses the best disc. Continue this process until one player throws his/her disc into the basket. Keep track of your team’s score for each course, elapsed play-time on each course, total score, and total elapsed time for all courses. After all participants have been submitted their scores from the challenge timeframe, results will be posted to the website.

Courses:

A suggested route of play route is included below. Suggested start time (no matter where you start) is dawn, as you will need all of the time you can get in order to complete the challenge!

**Remember that “The Village” is a private course. Before playing The Village, ensure you are a member or contact Tim Smook to discuss the daily rate fee for playing the course.**

  • White Bank/Pharaoh's Tomb (White Tees)
  • Ecoff/Goyne Park (Blue Tees)
  • Dorey Park (White Tees)
  • Gillies Creek (Red Tees)
  • New Hanover Church (Red Tees)
  • The Village
  • University of Richmond
  • Bryan Park (Yellow Tees except for Concrete Teepad on #16 and #18)

Feedback:

At the end of the challenge, along with submitting scores and elapsed time, participants will submit feedback about the course route and play experience that will be used to improve and expand RV8 Challenge in the years to come.
